I’ve been grappling with the side effects of upper management hyper focusing on, what i consider to be, the wrong metrics for most of this year. These range from the mildly irritating (defect rate) to downright absurd (defect rate per developer).

Two adages came to mind when reading this confirmation-bias inducing essay. The famed “Cobra problem” which is a classic case of a metric gone awry and making the situation worse, and the adage that when “a measure ceases to be useful when it becomes a target”
